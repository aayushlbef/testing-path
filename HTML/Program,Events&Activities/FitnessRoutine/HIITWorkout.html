High Intensity

HIIT Workout

Maximize your calorie burn in minimal time! High-Intensity Interval Training combines short bursts of intense exercise with brief recovery periods for optimal fat burning and cardiovascular fitness.

20 minutes per session
Gym Floor or Outdoor Track
Focus: Fat Burning & Cardio
Available Daily - Self-guided or Group Sessions

Sample Workout Structure

Warm-Up 3 minutes

Light jogging, jumping jacks, dynamic stretches

HIIT Rounds (4 cycles)
12 minutes
  • Burpees - 40 seconds Rest 20 seconds
  • Mountain Climbers - 40 seconds Rest 20 seconds
  • High Knees - 40 seconds Rest 20 seconds
  • Jump Squats - 40 seconds Rest 20 seconds
  • Bicycle Crunches - 40 seconds Rest 20 seconds
Cool Down 5 minutes

Walking, static stretching, deep breathing

Why HIIT Works

Afterburn Effect

Continue burning calories for hours after your workout through excess post-exercise oxygen consumption (EPOC).

Time Efficient

Get a full workout in just 20 minutes - perfect for busy student schedules between classes.

Metabolic Boost

Increase metabolism and improve cardiovascular health with regular HIIT training.

Tips for Success

Do's

  • Warm up properly before starting
  • Give maximum effort during work intervals
  • Stay hydrated throughout
  • Allow 48 hours recovery between HIIT sessions

Modifications

  • Start with 20-second work, 40-second rest if needed
  • Remove jumping movements for low-impact
  • Focus on form over speed
  • Listen to your body and rest when needed
