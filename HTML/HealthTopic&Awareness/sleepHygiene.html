Sleep Hygiene

Master the art of quality sleep for better health, focus, and academic performance.

Sleep is essential for learning, memory consolidation, and overall health. During sleep, your brain processes information from the day, forms new neural connections, and clears out toxins. For college students, adequate sleep is crucial for academic success and well-being.

Unfortunately, many students sacrifice sleep due to academic demands, social activities, and irregular schedules. Chronic sleep deprivation can lead to decreased concentration, impaired judgment, weakened immune function, and increased risk of mental health issues.

Understanding Sleep Cycles

A complete sleep cycle lasts about 90 minutes and repeats 4-6 times per night.

Stage 1 (Light Sleep)

1-5 min

Running, cycling, swimming - improves heart health and burns calories.

Stage 2 (Light Sleep)

10-60 min

Body temperature drops, heart rate slows

Stage 3 (Deep Sleep)

20-40 min

Most restorative sleep, tissue repair occurs

REM Sleep

10-60 min

Dreaming occurs, memory consolidation

Essential Sleep Hygiene Practices

Consistent Schedule

Go to bed and wake up at the same time every day, even on weekends.

Limit Screen Time

10-15 min

Avoid screens 1-2 hours before bed. Blue light disrupts melatonin production.

Watch Caffeine

Avoid caffeine 6 hours before bedtime to prevent sleep disruption.

Morning Sunlight

Get natural light exposure in the morning to regulate your circadian rhythm.

Optimize Your Sleep Environment

Temperature

Keep your room cool, ideally 60-67°F (15-19°C)

A cooler environment promotes better sleep quality

Darkness

Use blackout curtains or an eye mask

Darkness triggers melatonin production

Noise Control

Use earplugs or white noise if needed

Minimize disruptions from dorm noise

Comfortable Bedding

Invest in a supportive mattress and pillows

Physical comfort is essential for quality sleep

Create a Relaxing Bedtime Routine

Start your wind-down routine 30-60 minutes before bed:

1

Dim the Lights

Reduce bright light exposure to signal your brain it's time to sleep.

2

Put Away Devices

Stop using phones, tablets, and computers at least 1 hour before bed.

3

Relaxing Activities

Read a book, take a warm bath, practice gentle stretching, or listen to calm music.

4

Prepare for Tomorrow

Lay out clothes and pack your bag to reduce morning stress.

5

Practice Gratitude

Reflect on positive moments from your day to promote relaxation.

Overcoming Common Sleep Challenges

Trouble Falling Asleep

Solution: If you can't fall asleep after 20 minutes, get up and do a quiet, non-stimulating activity until you feel sleepy. Avoid watching the clock.

Waking Up During the Night

Solution: Avoid looking at screens. Practice deep breathing or progressive muscle relaxation to help you return to sleep.

Racing Thoughts

Solution: Keep a "worry journal" by your bed to write down concerns. Practice mindfulness meditation to quiet your mind.

Inconsistent Schedule

Solution: Gradually adjust your sleep schedule by 15-30 minutes each day. Stick to consistent times even on weekends.
