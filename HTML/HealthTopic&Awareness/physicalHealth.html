Physical Fitness

Build strength, endurance, and overall health through regular physical activity and exercise.

Why Exercise Matters

Regular physical activity is one of the most important things you can do for your health. Being physically active can improve your brain health, help manage weight, reduce the risk of disease, strengthen bones and muscles, and improve your ability to do everyday activities.

For college students, exercise offers additional benefits including improved concentration, better stress management, and enhanced academic performance. Just 150 minutes of moderate activity per week can make a significant difference.

Key Benefits of Regular Exercise

Improved cardiovascular health
Increased strength and endurance
Better mood and mental health
Enhanced sleep quality
Boosted immune system
Stress and anxiety reduction

Types of Exercise

Cardio Training

30-45 min

3-5x per week

Running, cycling, swimming - improves heart health and burns calories.

Strength Training

45-60 min

2-3x per week

Weight lifting, bodyweight exercises - builds muscle and increases metabolism.

Flexibility Work

15-30 min

Daily

Yoga, stretching - improves range of motion and prevents injury.

HIIT Workouts

20-30 min

2-3x per week

High-intensity intervals - efficient calorie burn and fitness gains.

Weekly Exercise Recommendations

150

Minutes Per Week

Moderate-intensity aerobic activity (e.g., brisk walking, cycling)

2+

Days Per Week

Muscle-strengthening activities involving all major muscle groups

Sample Beginner Weekly Plan

Day Activity Duration
Monday Cardio (jogging or cycling) 30 minutes
Tuesday Strength training (upper body) 45 minutes
Wednesday Active rest (yoga or stretching) 20 minutes
Thursday Cardio (swimming or dancing) 30 minutes
Friday Strength training (lower body) 45 minutes
Weekend Recreation (hiking, sports) 60+ minutes

Getting Started Tips

1

Start slowly and gradually increase intensity to avoid injury

2

Find activities you enjoy to make exercise sustainable

3

4

Use campus recreation facilities - they're free for students!

5
